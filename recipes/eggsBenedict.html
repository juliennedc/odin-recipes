Eggs Benedict

Description

Dubbed The Queen of Breakfast, this dish is a beautiful gem amongst the rubble of other egg dishes. Cooked to perfection by the tried-and-true classic recipe, Erina added a secret weapon to it: Karasumi powder to further enhance the egg theme. One bite will instinctively cause servility to Erina.

Ingredients

  • Ground Chive
  • Hollandaise Sauce
    • 3 Egg Yolks
    • 1/2 Tbsp Lemon Juice
    • 8 Tbsp Melted Butter
    • Water
  • Poached Egg
  • 4 Bacon Strips
  • 2 English Muffin
  • 1 Tsp Salt
    • Karasumi Powder
      • Dried Mullet Roe
      • Dried Various Fish Eggs

Steps

  1. Line bacon on a rack and place on baking sheet. Bake for 15 minutes at 400 degrees Fahrenheit. After bacon is done cooking, pat dry to remove excess fat/li>
  2. Place egg yolks in bowl with lemon juice. Whisk until fully combined. Bring bowl over a pot of simmering water (double boiler method) and continuously whisk until volume has increased ~1-2 minutes
  3. Slowly add melted butter into bowl and continuously whisk. Continue until all butter is used and sauce is thick. ~5-7 minutes
  4. Season with salt and cayenne pepper
  5. Crack egg into a bowl or ramekin
  6. Bring a pot of water to a simmer. Create a vortex by swirling spoon in water. Carefully place egg in. If doing more than one egg in one pot, carefully create a vortex after each egg
  7. After all eggs are in water, turn off the heat, cover, and let it cook for 1-2 minutes. Depending how thoroughly you'd like your yolk cooked
  8. Toast English muffin in preferred method
  9. To assemble: begin with english muffin, a dash of karasumi powder, place a bacon strip on top, then one poached egg, and finally drizzle hollandaise sauce over
  10. Optional: sprinkle furakake over the top
